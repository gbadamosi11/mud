About

Mud Art is a dance Company in Kaduna state Nigeria. It was started by its artistic director and resident choreographer Ochai Ogaba, the creator of mud dance. The Company is known for its maiden dance production titled “Mud”. Mud shares a Narrative of the creative process of the mud dance, which came up as a result of a group of dancers in northern Nigeria ( Kaduna states precisely) who at some stages of their lives suffered from a series of traumatic experinces like the insurgences in the North east and North western states in Nigeria, family feuds, sexual abuse and the secterian crisis that took the lives of thousands of the people from the region, it led most of them to depression and some mental disorders, Dance became a healing place and through these constant expressiveness they started exploring different forms of body movements that turned out to be quiet distinct from other dance styles, it led to the formation of the dance form “mud“. The company has performed in the Kaduna book and art festival, and it has successfully organized two productions. Mud in 2019 and Plug in 2020 respectively and was among the winners of the EUNIC Cluster Unity project in Nigeria in 2020. Ochai Ogaba describes Mud as a space to constantly evolve and explore, create and heal through authentic artistic expressions.

he REACH DANCE program is a schools (primary and secondary) program where we teach students/pupils how to dance, the aim is to build authentic artists by training them from the early stages of this art form, and to also prevent them from the risk of being exposed to drug abuse or the use of harmful substance or indecent exposure to sexual activities, we use this platform as an expressive space for them to explore their creativity in the performing arts. After a period of time the child is expected to develop certain attributes such as confidence, critical thinking skills, sense of belonging, cultural performance and creativity. this initiative was first introduced to the Demonstration School for Deaf Children (DSDC) Kawo Kaduna, Nigeria in 2018 by Ochai Ogaba as the teacher and it is now incorporated into the company as an outreach program.

Vision

Our vision is to create dance works in Africa that will tell the stories of its communities through compelling narratives, to create a safe space to constantly evolve and explore, and ultimately to bring healing to anyone and everyone who needs it.
