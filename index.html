The mud was premiered on 22nd, June 2019 at space 2000 Barnawa, kaduna. MUD The production shares a narrative of the creative process of mud dance, which came up as a result of a group of people (dancers in Northern Nigeria, Kaduna precisely) who at some stages of their lives suffered from series of stressful events like the insurgence of Boko Haram, sexual abuse, religious crisis all of which had led to a form of depression or any of the mental disorders. dance became a healing place for these dancers and through constant expressiveness of their lives they started exploring different forms of movements that turned out to be quiet distinct from other styles, this became hope and a healing tool for the dancers .The Mud dance is inspired from the substance Mud, which is a combination of other elements like earth and water. The emphasis is on the significance of mud in the being of man. It's ability to be formed and reformed, to create and serve, even to heal when the man is broken. We acknowledge this substance as part of our existence. We have spent time in research on the beauty of this substance and its relativity to the body enough to show our gratitude to its functionality through body movements and forms that appraises its memory.

The plug was premiered on 28th, November 2020 at space 2000 Barnawa, kaduna. 2020 was a challenging year for the entire world it revealed the vulnerability of not just one person or race but the entire human race, it revealed so much about what we all have in common, fear and anxiety pushed us all in the direction of what could possibly be, most of it was about surviving in the unknown, could this be the end of the world as many asked or could it be the universe trying to talk to us about what is really important. At some point everything was important, but this time the whole earth stood still and watched in the unknown. 2020 started off with the Covid 19 pandemic that took the lives of many, then followed by the black lives matter movement and relatively the end sars protest from Nigeria, this has shaped the entire world in a way that nothing will ever remain the same, at least for the next decade, not living out the 2020 presidential US elections. Reforms were made economics were Changed our lives indeed will never remain the same. The concept of plug comes from this chaotic moments of our lives and the possibility of moving even in a confined state , the need to connect and reunite with ourselves, listening to the stillness of the universe and what it has to say, drawing out beauty from one of the most terrifying times of all time. All we have at this point is these connections and in this connections we can build authentic relationships in our communities and create new families outside ourselves.

In 'Compound' dance production, we will explore compounds of rural settlements to confront the prevalence of global ehtno-religious wars, dissecting every bit of it and making an art out of it to share the narrative of 'Healing' from the sectarian crisis caused by the fight for space, ethnicity and religion. It will push the concept of cohesion, community and unity through dance. 'Compound' as a dance production will as such use the narrative to show how we all share a common space and we are all responsible for its wellness, equality and fairness
